H-1B Program — Major Changes Active
$100,000 Supplemental H-1B Fee
● Active Sep 21, 2025

Presidential Proclamation requires employers to pay a $100,000 supplemental fee per H-1B petition for beneficiaries outside the US or requiring consular processing. Must be paid via pay.gov before filing Form I-129 — failure results in automatic denial. Exemptions include F-1 status changes, extensions, amendments, and transfers for workers already in the US. Fee expires September 2026 unless extended. Multiple legal challenges filed; courts have declined to block it. Employers must plan as if it will remain in effect.

Active now Pay via pay.gov before filing Extensions exempt Legal challenge pending Sep 2026 expiry
Wage-Weighted H-1B Lottery
● Effective Feb 27, 2026

Replaces the random H-1B lottery with a wage-weighted selection process for FY2027 cap season. Higher-wage applicants receive greater selection probability — Level 1 entries counted once, Level 2 twice, Level 3 three times, Level 4 four times. Favors senior, higher-paid hires. Disadvantages entry-level and mid-level recruiting and smaller employers who cannot match large tech company salaries. Effective for the FY2027 H-1B cap registration season beginning March 2026.

Effective Feb 27, 2026 FY2027 cap season Wage-level weighting Favors senior hires USCIS final rule
Social Media Vetting — All H-1B & H-4 Applicants
⚡ Active Dec 15, 2025

As of December 15, 2025, all H-1B and H-4 visa applicants are subject to mandatory social media vetting — regardless of nationality. Applicants must set social media profiles to public. Consular officers review résumés, LinkedIn pages, and trade publication articles. Includes dependents on H-4 visas. Expect longer processing times for visa stamping. Employers should notify affected employees, update travel policies, and plan for potential extended absences abroad.

Active Dec 15, 2025 All nationalities Includes H-4 dependents LinkedIn reviewed Longer processing times
EAD Auto-Extension Eliminated
⚡ Active Oct 30, 2025

The 540-day automatic extension for timely EAD renewal filings has been eliminated for most categories as of October 30, 2025. Employees lose work authorization the day after their EAD expires unless a new card has been issued — they cannot work until the new EAD is received. Maximum EAD validity also reduced to 18 months (from 5 years) for refugees, asylees, adjustment of status applicants, and others. Employers must proactively track EAD expirations and begin renewal processes significantly earlier than before.

Active Oct 30, 2025 No auto-extension 18mo max validity Track expirations proactively Work gap risk
Enforcement & Compliance
Project Firewall — Expanded FDNS Enforcement
● Active & Escalating

48% jump in Department of Labor probes targeting H-1B fraud and compliance. FDNS (Fraud Detection and National Security) site visits significantly expanded. Employers must ensure job duties in petitions accurately reflect real-world responsibilities, wage levels match actual compensation, worksites and supervisory structures are correctly documented, and third-party placements are properly monitored. Non-compliant employers face penalties, revocations, and debarment from the H-1B program.

+48% DOL probes FDNS site visits up Job duty accuracy critical Third-party placement scrutiny
H-1B & L-1 Visa Reform Act of 2025 (Proposed)
◎ Monitor — Legislative

Proposed legislation would increase employer reporting and evidentiary requirements, raise penalties for violations, introduce annual DHS compliance audits, and tighten L-1 "specialized knowledge" definitions. L-1 authorization would be limited to one year unless employer demonstrates no US worker displacement. Not yet enacted — monitor progress as it may affect FY2027 and beyond planning.

Proposed — not enacted Annual DHS audits L-1 changes included Increased penalties

CRITICAL
Proactively track all EAD expirations
With auto-extension eliminated, employees lose work authorization on the day their EAD expires if a new card hasn't been issued. Build a 6-month advance renewal trigger into your immigration tracking system. Any gap in work authorization creates an I-9 compliance violation.
CRITICAL
Brief employees on social media vetting
All H-1B and H-4 employees who will travel internationally for visa stamping must set social media profiles to public. Brief employees and dependents. Update travel approval process to verify compliance before any international travel that requires visa stamping.

Common employer-sponsored visa types
H-1B — Specialty Occupation

Most common tech visa. Requires bachelor's degree in specialty field. 65K annual cap + 20K for US advanced degrees. 3-year initial, extendable to 6 years. Now subject to $100K fee for new consular petitions and wage-weighted lottery.

L-1 — Intracompany Transferee

For multinational transfers — managers (L-1A) and specialized knowledge workers (L-1B). No cap. Requires 1 year employment abroad with same company in past 3 years. L-1B "specialized knowledge" definition tightening under proposed reform legislation.

OPT / STEM OPT — F-1 Students

12 months post-graduation work authorization, extendable to 24 additional months for STEM degrees. No employer sponsorship cost. Critical pipeline for tech companies. EAD-based work authorization — subject to new 18-month max validity and no auto-extension.

TN — NAFTA/USMCA (Canada & Mexico)

Available to Canadian and Mexican citizens in qualifying professional roles (engineers, scientists, accountants). No annual cap, faster processing, no lottery. Increasingly valuable alternative to H-1B for qualifying candidates from Canada and Mexico.

EB-2 / EB-3 — Employment-Based Green Card

PERM labor certification required for most EB-2/EB-3 cases. Long backlogs for India and China-born applicants (10–50+ years for some categories). EB-1A/EB-1B (extraordinary ability) bypasses PERM. Key retention tool for long-term H-1B holders.

Key concepts for HR leaders
LCA — Labor Condition Application
Filed with DOL before H-1B petition. Employer attests to prevailing wage, working conditions, and no strike/lockout. Accuracy is critical — FDNS verifies these against actual job duties.
PERM — Permanent Labor Certification
DOL process required for most employment-based green cards. Requires employer to prove no qualified US workers available. Processing times: 12–24+ months. Critical to start early for H-1B employees approaching 6-year cap.
Cap-Exempt H-1B
Universities, nonprofits, and government research organizations are exempt from the 65K cap. Some employers partner with cap-exempt entities to employ workers outside the lottery.
Priority Date
The date a PERM or I-140 is filed — determines queue position for green card availability. India and China-born applicants face multi-decade backlogs in EB-2/EB-3 categories.
H-4 EAD
Work authorization for H-1B spouses who are in the green card process. Subject to same EAD auto-extension elimination. Track separately — H-4 EAD holders losing work authorization mid-project is a significant operational risk.
