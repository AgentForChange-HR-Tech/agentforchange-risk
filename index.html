US State Laws
California Civil Rights Council — ADS Regulations
● Live

Effective October 1, 2025. Extends California's anti-discrimination laws to automated decision systems (ADS) in employment. Requires meaningful human oversight with override capability, proactive bias testing, 4-year data retention, and vendor liability under agency principles.

Effective Oct 2025 Bias testing required 4yr data retention Vendor liability Human oversight
Illinois HB 3773 — AI in Employment Decisions
● Live

Effective January 1, 2026. Amends the Illinois Human Rights Act to prohibit discriminatory AI use in hiring, promotion, discipline, and termination. Requires advance notice to applicants when AI influences covered employment decisions. Employers remain responsible for AI outcomes even from third-party vendors.

Effective Jan 2026 Notice required Anti-discrimination Vendor accountability
Colorado AI Act (SB 24-205)
⚡ Jun 30, 2026

One of the most comprehensive US state AI laws. Regulates "high-risk" AI in employment decisions — hiring, firing, promotion. Requires annual impact assessments, transparency when AI is used, and foreseeable risk disclosures. Currently subject to federal legal challenge by DOJ and xAI, but compliance preparation is advised regardless. Violations constitute unfair trade practice.

Deadline Jun 30, 2026 Annual impact assessment High-risk classification Under legal challenge Unfair trade practice
Texas TRAIGA — Responsible AI Governance Act
● Live

Effective January 1, 2026. Requires developers and deployers of high-risk AI to use reasonable care to prevent algorithmic discrimination. Establishes a framework targeting discriminatory outcomes in employment, credit, and housing decisions.

Effective Jan 2026 Anti-discrimination Developer & deployer liability
NYC Local Law 144 — Automated Employment Decision Tools
● Live

Requires independent annual bias audits before using automated employment decision tools to screen NYC candidates or employees. Employers must provide advance notice and disclose job qualifications the tool evaluates. Applies to tools that substantially assist or replace discretionary hiring decisions.

Annual bias audit Candidate notice NYC employers Third-party audit
EU & Global
EU AI Act — High-Risk HR Systems
⚡ Aug 2, 2026

The world's first comprehensive AI regulation. AI used in recruitment, screening, selection, performance evaluation, or promotion is explicitly classified as high-risk under Annex III. Full compliance required by August 2, 2026. Extraterritorial reach — applies to any employer whose AI outputs affect EU candidates or employees, regardless of HQ location. Penalties up to €35M or 7% of global turnover.

Deadline Aug 2, 2026 Extraterritorial reach Human oversight required Bias testing mandatory €35M max fine Worker notice required
EU AI Act — Prohibited Practices (in effect)
● Live Feb 2025

Banned practices took effect February 2, 2025. Emotion recognition in the workplace is absolutely prohibited. Biometric categorization based on sensitive traits is banned. AI literacy obligations now require employers to train staff involved in AI operations on how AI works and governance responsibilities.

Emotion recognition banned Biometric categorization banned AI literacy required In effect now

Critical actions — do now
CRITICAL
Audit your AI tool inventory
Identify every tool in your HR and recruiting stack that uses algorithmic processing — ATS ranking, sourcing AI, video interview scoring, scheduling bots, performance analytics. Document whether each vendor classifies their tool as high-risk.
CRITICAL
Confirm EU AI Act exposure
If your company recruits EU candidates, employs EU-based workers, or deploys global HR platforms used by EU teams — you are in scope for the EU AI Act regardless of where your HQ is located. August 2, 2026 deadline.
CRITICAL
Establish human-in-the-loop controls
Required by California (Oct 2025), EU AI Act, and recommended under EEOC guidance. A qualified person must be able to understand, question, and override AI outputs at all employment decision points.
CRITICAL
Build notice & disclosure workflows
Illinois (Jan 2026) and NYC Local Law 144 require advance notice to candidates when AI influences employment decisions. Colorado (Jun 2026) requires transparency disclosures. Build triggered notice workflows into your ATS or recruiting process.
High priority — complete before Q3 2026
HIGH
Conduct bias testing on AI hiring tools
Required under California, Colorado, EU AI Act, and NYC Local Law 144. Test AI tools for disparate impact across gender, race, age, and other protected classes. Document results and remediation steps.
HIGH
Establish 4-year data retention for ADS records
California requires retention of input data, outputs, criteria used, and bias testing results for automated decision systems for a minimum of 4 years. Begin data governance framework now.
HIGH
Review vendor contracts for AI Act compliance
Request compliance documentation, bias test reports, and technical files from every HR AI vendor. Ensure contracts define vendor liability for regulatory violations. Under California and EU rules, employers bear responsibility for vendor AI failures.
HIGH
Assign internal AI compliance ownership
Designate who owns AI governance in HR — typically a cross-functional role spanning HR, Legal, IT, and People Tech. Define accountability for bias monitoring, human oversight design, and compliance tracking.
MEDIUM
Train HR team on AI literacy requirements
EU AI Act Article 4 requires AI literacy training for all staff involved in AI operations. Ensure HR team understands how your AI tools work, their limitations, and how to manage them responsibly.

Key frameworks & concepts
Disparate Impact
When AI produces discriminatory outcomes against protected classes, even without discriminatory intent. Core Title VII risk for AI hiring tools.
High-Risk AI Classification
EU AI Act Annex III explicitly classifies all recruitment, screening, selection, and performance AI as high-risk — triggering mandatory compliance obligations.
Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)
Required by multiple laws — a qualified person must be able to understand, question, and override AI employment decisions.
Brussels Effect
EU AI Act expected to set global compliance standard, similar to GDPR. Multinationals will likely apply EU standards globally.
Deployer vs Provider Liability
Both tool vendors (providers) and the employers who use them (deployers) face liability under EU AI Act and US state laws. Vendor contracts must define accountability.
